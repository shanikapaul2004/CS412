About Walt Disney

Biography

Walt Disney (1901–1966) was an American animator, film producer, and entrepreneur. He is best known for creating some of the most iconic characters in entertainment history, including Mickey Mouse, and for founding Disneyland and Walt Disney World. His innovations in animation and theme parks transformed popular culture worldwide.

Throughout his career, Disney worked as a voice actor, producer, and visionary studio head, pioneering synchronized sound and Technicolor in animated films. He was instrumental in shaping the modern entertainment industry and built one of the world’s largest media empires.

Disney received 22 Academy Awards and 59 nominations, more than any other individual in history. In 1964, he was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Lyndon B. Johnson.
